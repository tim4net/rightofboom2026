Executive Summary

Across 3 endpoints, the validation results indicate widespread gaps in malware prevention, credential protection, execution controls, and logging—creating a high likelihood that a common phishing or malware event could quickly escalate to credential theft and lateral movement. Because nearly every control tested is failing across all endpoints, the environment is currently positioned for rapid attacker progression with limited detection and response visibility.

Critical Findings

EICAR Malware Detection
Why this matters: If the endpoint security stack cannot reliably detect a known-safe test signature, real commodity malware delivered via email attachment, drive-by download, or USB is more likely to execute undetected. Attack scenario: a user opens a malicious attachment; the payload runs, establishes persistence, and begins credential theft and lateral movement before anyone is alerted. Business risk includes ransomware, data theft, and operational downtime.
Application Control (AppLocker/WDAC)
Why this matters: Without application control, users (or malware) can run untrusted binaries and scripts from common locations (Downloads, Temp, user profile paths). Attack scenario: an attacker delivers a signed-but-abused tool or a living-off-the-land dropper that executes freely, then stages additional payloads and persistence. Business risk is increased likelihood of ransomware execution and reduced ability to contain initial access.
ASR Rules Enforcement
Why this matters: Attack Surface Reduction rules are designed to block common malware behaviors (macro abuse, credential theft patterns, suspicious child processes). Attack scenario: a phishing document spawns PowerShell or a script interpreter; without ASR enforcement, the chain proceeds to download and execute payloads. Business risk includes higher infection rates and faster attacker progression from initial access to impact.
Credential Guard
Why this matters: Credential Guard helps protect cached and in-memory credentials from theft. Attack scenario: after initial compromise, an attacker dumps credentials from memory and reuses them to access file shares, servers, and cloud resources. Business risk includes domain-wide compromise, data exfiltration, and ransomware propagation.
LSASS Protected Process
Why this matters: If LSASS is not protected, credential dumping becomes significantly easier. Attack scenario: an attacker runs a credential dumping tool and extracts password hashes or plaintext credentials, then pivots to other systems. Business risk includes rapid escalation to privileged accounts and broad compromise.
SMB Signing Required
Why this matters: Without SMB signing, attackers can more easily perform man-in-the-middle or relay-style attacks against SMB authentication and traffic. Attack scenario: an attacker on the same network relays captured authentication to access shares or execute remote actions. Business risk includes lateral movement, data exposure, and faster spread of ransomware across file shares.
SMB Signing Required (Lateral Movement Prevention)
Why this matters: Lateral movement commonly uses SMB for remote access and file operations. If SMB signing is not required, it increases the success rate of credential relay and session hijacking techniques. Business risk includes attackers moving from one endpoint to many with minimal resistance.
BitLocker Drive Encryption
Why this matters: Without full disk encryption, lost or stolen devices can expose sensitive data (email, cached files, browser sessions, tokens). Attack scenario: a laptop is lost during travel; an attacker extracts data from the drive offline. Business risk includes reportable data breach, regulatory exposure, and reputational damage.
PowerShell Script Block Logging
Why this matters: PowerShell is a common attacker tool for execution and post-exploitation. Without script block logging, malicious commands may run with little forensic trace. Attack scenario: an attacker uses PowerShell to download payloads and enumerate the environment; defenders lack the telemetry to detect or investigate. Business risk includes delayed detection and increased incident scope/cost.
Process Command Line Logging
Why this matters: Command-line visibility is critical for detecting suspicious execution (encoded commands, LOLBIN abuse, unusual parent/child process chains). Attack scenario: malware launches system tools with malicious parameters; without command-line logs, detection rules and investigations lose key context. Business risk includes missed detections and inability to reconstruct attacker activity.
Defender Exclusions
Why this matters: Excessive or unsafe AV exclusions create blind spots where malware can run without scanning. Attack scenario: an attacker drops payloads into excluded paths or uses excluded processes to avoid detection. Business risk includes persistent undetected malware and higher ransomware success probability.
LOLBIN Control (AppLocker/WDAC)
Why this matters: Living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBINs) are legitimate Windows tools frequently abused to evade defenses. Without controls, attackers can use built-in tools to download, execute, and persist with fewer alerts. Business risk includes stealthier attacks and reduced effectiveness of traditional antivirus-only approaches.
